## 2 · Book 1, Chapter 3 — *What the heavens are and what their substance is*  *(p. 30)*

A short, dense cosmological preface that licenses everything the magic does.

**Doctrine, in brief.**
- **The sphere is perfect.** Heaven is spherical, round and smooth; the circle/sphere is the first and most perfect figure, made from a single line. The sky was never otherwise *(B1 Ch3, p.30)*.
- **No generation or corruption** occurs in the heavens; their superiority is why earthly change cannot touch them.
- **Rays from the centre.** From the centre of the sphere — which coincides with the centre of the **earth** — equal lines run to the surrounding circle. These lines are read as the **rays the stars cast upon the earth**, and *"from these come the power and virtue of images."* This is the physical premise of the whole art: talismans work because celestial rays converge on the centre where the operator stands.
- **360 degrees = 360 figures.** Heaven's first division yields 360 degrees and exactly as many figures; the judgments of astrology follow these images, and the heavens cause all operations below.
- **Heat and motion** in the world derive from the heavens.

### 2.1 · The elemental qualities of the planets

The chapter then gives the working temperaments — the passage that opens this whole study. Each body moves things of its own quality; the **Sun draws out and amplifies** a planet's virtue; a planet acting *with* its nature is stronger, *against* it weaker *(B1 Ch3, p.30)*.

| Body | Quality (per the text) | Element | Role |
|------|------------------------|---------|------|
| Saturn | cold and dry | Earth | greater infortune |
| Jupiter | warm and moist | Air | greater fortune; temperate |
| Mars | hot and dry | Fire | lesser infortune; burning |
| Sun | hot and dry — **amplifier** | Fire | *draws out & increases* a planet's virtue |
| Venus | moderately hot, very moist | Air/Water | lesser fortune |
| Mercury | weakly hot, very dry | Earth/Air | convertible |
| Moon | cold and moist | Water | governs all works beneath her |
| Fixed stars | as the Moon (cold & moist) | Water | move the same things as Luna |

## 3 · Book 1, Chapter 4 — *The general theory and arrangement of the heavens for making magical images*  *(pp. 31–38)*

The practical heart of Book One: **how to time an image**. It has three parts — the foundations, the 28 Mansions of the Moon, and the rule of election.

### 3.1 · Foundations of image-magic
- **Master the calculation of positions.** Anyone who would make images must first know how to calculate the positions of the planets and the constellations and the motions of the heavens *(B1 Ch4, p.31)*.
- **Believe firmly.** The work is not done to test whether it is true; the operator must hold it true. This strengthens the rational spirit and joins it to the celestial virtue that acts in the image. The human soul is described as the lowest of the intelligences that can call on the cooperation of the higher ones.
- **Watch the Moon above all.** Do nothing unless the Moon stands in a degree fit for the work, *"because the Moon has powers and manifest works in all things that are beneath her."*

### 3.3 · The rule of election (timing the Moon)

For **benefic** works, keep these conditions; for **malefic** works the text says to do the contrary *(B1 Ch4, pp. 31–38, with fn 51–54)*.

| Factor | For benefic works | Source |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| Moon dignity | well dignified & strong | Ch4 |
| Saturn & Mars (infortunes) | safe from them and their aspects | Ch4; fn53 |
| Combustion / Sun's rays | free of the beams (> 12° from the Sun) | Ch4 |
| Jupiter & Venus (fortunes) | joined by trine or sextile — separating from one, applying to the other | Ch4; fn51 |
| Via Combusta | avoid 8° Libra – 3° Scorpio | Ch4; fn54 |
| Moon's speed | swift; **< 12°/day is "like Saturn"** and to be avoided | Ch4 |
| Eclipse | not applying to an eclipse | Ch4 |
| End of sign | avoid the last degrees (terms of the infortunes) | Ch4 |
| Cadency | not cadent from the midheaven (the 9th house) | Ch4 |
| Day vs. night | day-work: Moon ascending in a **diurnal** sign; night-work: a **nocturnal** sign | Ch4 |
| Sign of ascension | short-ascension signs (Cap–Gem) = swifter; long-ascension = slower | Ch4; fn52 |
| Remedy if unavoidable | set **Jupiter and/or Venus on the Ascendant or Midheaven** to rectify an afflicted Moon | Ch4 |

**Key definitions (from the footnotes).** *Fortunes* = Jupiter & Venus (fn51). *Infortunes* = Saturn & Mars (fn53). *Short/direct ascension* = Capricorn, Aquarius, Pisces, Aries, Taurus, Gemini (fn52). *Via Combusta* = 8° Libra to 3° Scorpio (fn54). *Humane signs* = Gemini, Virgo, Libra, Sagittarius, Aquarius (named at Mansion 5).

## 4 · The calculation engine & how it was verified

The companion files compute the sky for any moment and apply the Chapter-4 rules.

**What it does.**
1. **Planetary positions** — geocentric, tropical, of-date longitudes for the Sun, Moon, and planets.
2. **Sign, mansion & dignity** — sign and degree, the Moon's mansion (360 ÷ 28), and **William Lilly's essential dignities** (domicile +5, exaltation +4, triplicity +3, term +2, face +1; detriment −5, fall −4), using the Egyptian terms and Chaldean faces.
3. **Electional checklist** — combustion / under-the-rays (12°), aspects to Saturn/Mars and to Jupiter/Venus (with applying vs. separating), the Via Combusta, the Moon's speed, an approximate ascendant, and day/night by the Sun's altitude.

**Astronomy.** Positions use **Paul Schlyter's analytic ephemeris** (accuracy ≈ 1–4′), chosen because it needs no external data and runs identically in Python and the browser. A production build could substitute the Swiss Ephemeris behind the same interface.

**Astrology.** Dignities, aspects, combustion, and the Via Combusta follow the Ptolemaic/Lilly tradition of *Christian Astrology* (1647).

**Verification performed.**
- The 28 mansions **tile 360° exactly** (no gaps or overlaps); lookup confirmed at boundaries (0° Aries → M1, 13° Aries → M2, 30° Pisces → M28).
- Lilly's dignity tables are internally consistent (each sign's terms reach 30°; detriment = ruler of the opposite sign; three faces per sign), with spot-checks (Sun 19° Aries → exaltation + triplicity; Mars in Libra → detriment).
- The ephemeris was **cross-checked** against independently confirmed positions for **2026-06-25 00:47 UT** (Sun 3°31′ Cancer, Jupiter 28°55′ Cancer, Saturn 13°47′ Aries, Mars 27°18′ Taurus, Venus 13°24′ Leo, Mercury 25°25′ Cancer, Moon 9°06′ Scorpio → Mansion 18, Alcalb). All matched.
- The **Python, standalone JavaScript, and in-page website engines all produce identical results** for that reference moment.
